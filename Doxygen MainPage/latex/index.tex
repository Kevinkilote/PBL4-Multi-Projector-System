\doxysubsection*{Alpha blending}

 \tabulinesep=1mm
\begin{longtabu}spread 0pt [c]{*{2}{|X[-1]}|}
\hline
{\bfseries{Description\+:}}  &The Alpha Blending technique, implemented in the code, is used to combine two images, typically from different sources or angles, in a smooth and gradual manner. It involves the manipulation of transparency levels for each pixel in the images to create a seamless transition effect when overlaying one image over another.   \\\cline{1-2}
{\bfseries{Formula\+:}}  &

For left\+: \[  R = (1 - \alpha) \cdot CL + \alpha \cdot RL \] For right\+: \[  R = CR + \alpha \cdot RR \cdot (1 - \alpha)  \]



\\\cline{1-2}
\end{longtabu}

\doxysubsection*{Intensity modifier}

 \tabulinesep=1mm
\begin{longtabu}spread 0pt [c]{*{2}{|X[-1]}|}
\hline
{\bfseries{Description\+:}}  &The Intensity Modifier plays a critical role in creating smooth transitions and blending effects in the final image, ensuring that the overlay of two images appears seamless and visually pleasing. It controls the pixel intensity based on the image side and position within the mask.   \\\cline{1-2}
{\bfseries{Formula\+:}}  &

For left\+: \[  IF = 1.0 - \frac{mask - col}{mask} \] For right\+: \[  IF = 1.0 - \frac{col}{mask} \]
